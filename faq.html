Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about TikuOS, its design, and how to get involved.

General

What is TikuOS?

TikuOS is an open-source operating system designed for ultra-low-power embedded devices. It targets physical AI endpoints that need to run for years on a coin cell battery or indefinitely on harvested energy.

What does "Tiku" mean?

Tiku is derived from a word meaning "small" or "tiny" — reflecting the project's focus on minimal, lightweight intelligence embedded into everyday objects.

How is TikuOS different from other embedded operating systems?

Unlike conventional RTOS platforms that assume milliwatt-level power budgets, TikuOS is built from the ground up for microwatt operation. It is fully event-driven, energy-aware, and natively supports sub-conventional radios like backscatter and tunnel diode transmitters.

What license is TikuOS released under?

TikuOS is fully open source under the Apache License 2.0.

Hardware & Compatibility

What hardware does TikuOS support?

TikuOS targets microcontrollers with kilobytes of RAM and flash. Specific board support packages and supported platforms will be listed in the documentation as they become available.

What communication technologies does TikuOS support?

TikuOS natively supports backscatter communication, tunnel diode transmitters, and other radios that operate below the power floor of conventional wireless. Support for standard protocols is planned.

Can TikuOS run on battery-free devices?

Yes. TikuOS is designed for devices powered by energy harvesting — solar, RF, thermal, or mechanical — and can operate indefinitely without a battery.

How can I contribute to TikuOS?

We welcome contributions of all kinds — code, documentation, bug reports, and hardware ports. Start by exploring the GitHub repository, opening an issue, or submitting a pull request.

Who maintains TikuOS?

TikuOS is maintained by Professor Ambuj Varshney and the WEISER Research Group, building on over a decade of research across three continents.

Please use the GitHub issue tracker to report bugs or suggest new features.
